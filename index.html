How will our cities and interactions change as we enter the fourth industrial revolution - the integration of our digital and physical worlds? We have seen such change begin in the past decade with the ubiquitousness of mobile devices allowing for an increase in data that can be collected, analyzed, repackaged, and ultimately predicted upon. Technology continues to fundamentally alter how we live, work, and socialize while some breakthroughs are on the cusp of entering the mainstream. Machine learning and autonomous robotics are increasing in use, while billions of IoT enabled devices are coming online. Nascent platforms like spatial computing and quantum computing show great promise in helping us find new ways to solve problems.
